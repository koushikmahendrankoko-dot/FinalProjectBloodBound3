💡 Inspiration & Design Goals

Bloodbound started as a simple question: what if your health bar was also your mana bar? Most RPGs treat HP as a resource to protect and mana/energy as a resource to spend. We wanted to collapse those two into one — forcing players into every fight as a genuine risk-reward calculation.

The primary inspirations were The Legend of Zelda (overworld exploration, dungeon structure, item discovery), Hades (fast combat, blood/sacrifice themes), and early Pokémon games (NPC dialogue, pixel world design, chapter progression).

The dark red visual identity was chosen deliberately to represent blood — not in a gratuitous way, but as a symbol of the game's core mechanic. The blood you spend to fight is the same blood that keeps you alive. Every ability is a gamble, and the visual design should make you feel that weight.

We built everything — the tile renderer, camera system, particle effects, dialogue engine, save system, enemy AI — entirely from scratch using the Canvas API and vanilla JavaScript. No game engines. No libraries. Just code.

🛠 Technical Highlights

🎮

Custom Game Loop

60fps requestAnimationFrame loop with delta time calculation for consistent gameplay across all devices.

🗺

Tile Map Renderer

JSON-based tilemap system with collision detection, multiple layers (floor, walls, overlay), and smooth camera scrolling.

🤖

Enemy AI

State-machine based AI (patrol → detect → chase → attack → retreat). Boss AI with multiple phases and pattern changes.

✨

Particle System

Custom particle engine for blood splatter, ability effects, hit sparks, and ambient environment particles.

💾

Save System

localStorage-based save system preserving HP, chapter progress, inventory, shop purchases, and story flags.

🎨

Sprite Animation

Spritesheet-based animation system with named states (idle, walk, attack, hurt, death) and frame timing control.
